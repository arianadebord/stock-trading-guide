Stock Market Dictionary

Ask

The lowest price someone is willing to sell for.

Bid

The highest price someone is willing to buy for.

Breakout

When price moves above an important resistance area.

Broker

The platform or company used to buy and sell stocks.

Bull Market

A market that is rising or expected to rise.

Bear Market

A market that is falling or expected to fall.

Candlestick

A chart candle that shows open, close, high, and low price.

Dividend

Money some companies pay shareholders.

ETF

A fund that holds a group of stocks or assets.

FOMO

Fear of missing out. Buying because you feel rushed.

Liquidity

How easily something can be bought or sold.

Portfolio

All the investments a person owns.

Resistance

A price area where a stock struggles to move above.

Stop Loss

A planned exit price if the trade goes against you.

Support

A price area where a stock struggles to fall below.

Volatility

How much a stock moves up and down.

Volume

The number of shares traded.
